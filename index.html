A charity organization, also known as a non-profit, is an entity dedicated to charitable purposes, primarily through fundraising and donations to support a cause. These organizations focus on philanthropy and social well-being, benefiting the public interest by providing assistance, relief, or serving public good, as described by Wikipedia. They offer a variety of services, including direct aid, information dissemination, and raising awareness about relevant issues.

Charitable organizations often focus on specific areas like poverty alleviation, education, healthcare, or environmental protection, among others. They are not-for-profit, meaning any revenue generated is reinvested into their mission and programs, not distributed as profits. Many charitable organizations are registered, allowing them to receive tax-deductible donations and utilize them for charitable purposes. These organizations play a vital role in society, addressing social needs and humanitarian challenges. They bring together individuals who care about a specific cause, enabling them to make a difference through their collective efforts. Charities are diverse in size and focus, with some specializing in local communities while others operate globally, according to How charities work.
